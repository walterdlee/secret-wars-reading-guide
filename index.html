About This Saga

An Epic Five Years
in the Making

Beginning in 2010 with S.H.I.E.L.D., Jonathan Hickman embarked on one of the most ambitious long-form narratives in Marvel's history. Over five years, he quietly wove together a vast tapestry — threading through secret societies, the fall of the Fantastic Four, the formation of the Illuminati, and the birth of a new Avengers — all converging on a single, inevitable endpoint.

What separates Hickman's run is its architectural patience. Seeds planted in early S.H.I.E.L.D. issues bloom chapters later. Characters in Fantastic Four carry grief into Avengers. The incursions that begin as background noise become an existential countdown. Every arc is a piece of the same machine — reading in order reveals a masterwork of long-form storytelling that rewards the committed reader completely.

It all culminates in Secret Wars (2015) — a collision of every Marvel universe, the payoff for every thread laid across five years. Widely regarded as one of the most structurally daring event comics ever published, and entirely earned by everything that came before.

"Everything dies. You. Me. Everyone on this planet. Our sun. Our galaxy. And, eventually, the universe itself. This is simply how things are."

— Jonathan Hickman, New Avengers #1
